The Mole Concept

Limiting Reagent

The limiting reagent is the reactant that is completely consumed first, determining how much product can form. The other reactant(s) are in excess.

How to Identify the Limiting Reagent

  1. Convert all reactant masses to moles using n = m/M
  2. Compare the mole ratio to the stoichiometric ratio from the balanced equation
  3. The reactant that would run out first is the limiting reagent

For our reaction: The ratio is 1 Zn : 2 MnO‚ÇÇ. If you have equal moles of each, MnO‚ÇÇ would be limiting because you need twice as much of it.
