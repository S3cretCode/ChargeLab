Physics: Current to Magnetic Fields

Understanding how moving charges (electric current) create magnetic fields.

Electric Current as Moving Charge

Electric current is the flow of electric charge, typically electrons moving through a conductor. Current (I) is measured in Amperes (A), where 1 A = 1 Coulomb per second.

The Right-Hand Rule

The right-hand rule is a mnemonic for determining the direction of the magnetic field relative to the current direction.

For Straight Wires

  1. Point your thumb in the direction of current flow (conventional current)
  2. Your fingers curl in the direction of the magnetic field lines
  3. The field forms concentric circles around the wire

For Loops and Solenoids

  1. Curl your fingers in the direction current flows around the loop
  2. Your thumb points in the direction of the magnetic field (through the center)
  3. Your thumb points toward the North pole
